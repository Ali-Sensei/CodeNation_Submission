Risks That Might Impact Digital Projects

To use digital technology without care and consideration to the risks involved is asking for failure. As with most things, care needs to be taken with technology in order to help it best serve us in the most productive way.

Unauthorized Access

It is highly likely with the focus on collaboration during digital projects, a lot of the files will be available on the cloud/on the internet. Unfortunately, due to hacking, it may be possible for others to access highly sensitive information that you may wish to keep private. It is even possible for someone who doesn’t know what they are doing to upload sensitive data and make it public because of a setting that they aren’t aware of or uploaded without reading the documentation to keep data secure.

Hardware Failure

While digital storage is convenient because it allows for storing files on disks that are extremely portable, what happens when those disks fail? What happens when a computer with the only backup of data suddenly experiences hardware failure. All that information is lost. It is a heart-breaking moment especially when personal information is lost, but the impact on a business when important data is lost can be extremely costly and can even cause businesses to fail. The importance of backup solutions cannot be overstated. Think about an automated database system that keeps track of customer information, and then that information is lost. For a business, it would be like losing all of their customers overnight.

Monetary Concerns

Technology can be expensive, not only to set up the hardware itself, but also to employ knowledgeable members whose job it is to ensure that the technology is running smoothly. Because of the large number of resources and how easy it is to get them, it is quite easy for a business to overspend on outside resources and eventually run out of money. It is a fact that most businesses fail due to cash flow issues, and with the heavy emphasis on technology, it is quite possible that businesses are spending money on technology that they don’t need.

Lack of Knowledge

There’s no denying that technology is being updated all the time. New technologies are coming out, new features, new ideas, new applications...these all require one to learn them in order to use them in the right way. Unfortunately, as technology changes, it’s likely that without proper education and re-training, a business may lose its grasp on current technology and become obsolete.
